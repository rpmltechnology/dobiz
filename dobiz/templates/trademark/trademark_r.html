Stepwise Procedure for Trademark Registration in India

The procedure of registering a trademark is easy and needs to be done in the manner that is prescribed. We'll assist you through the entire process, from submitting your trademark application to getting approved, so you don't miss a beat.

1. Filing of Trademark Application

The trademark application must be submitted within 24 hours after filing with the proper documentation and payment. The trademark application process is entirely online, and an instant acknowledgment is given immediately after filing the form. Once an acknowledgment confirmation, the applicant can begin to use TM in conjunction with the registered trademark.

2. The processing of applications at the Trademark Office

When the application is filed, the application is carefully examined to find any errors. If there are any errors following the evaluation after the evaluation, the TM office will either mark it for further review or announce that the submission has been rejected. The mistakes must be rectified in both cases.

3. Examining of Trademark Application

A trademark examiner reviews applications for trademark registration to assess if the trademark qualifies to be registered. After reviewing the trademark application, the examiner issues an examination report detailing his conclusions.

4. Respond to Examiner's Report

The trademark owner must respond to the examiner's report within thirty days after receipt. A response report of an examiner is a crucial drafting task that could either aid or hurt the applicant's trademark. We encourage applicants to help by giving us all the information and documents they can to help us prepare the most detailed and thorough reply to the Examination Report.

5. Show Cause Hearing

If receiving a written response to the exam report, the examiner is either content with the response or requests an inquiry into show cause. The outcome of the hearing determines the outcome of your Trademark application.

6. Publication Trademark

Trademark registration is made public in the journal after the examiner has been satisfied that the trademark is legal. This can be done in the exam stage or during the hearing for show cause, depending on the state of the request. Third parties can oppose the trademark registration, but if no objections are not received within 90 days of the date of the hearing, the registration process will be moved to the next step.

7. Presentation of the Trademark Registration Certificate

The next step is the application for registration by the registered agent. Once the registrar has successfully registered the trademark and issued the certificate of Trademark Registration, valid for 10 years from the date of issue. The trademarks need to be renewed every ten years to prolong the validity period.
