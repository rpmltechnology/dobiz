Provident Fund (PF) In India, registration is required

The registration of an establishment within the Provident Fund Organization is mandatory when the there are employees within the company exceeds 20 in total. To determine the legal limit of two even contracts, employees count separately from those employed through an affiliate company. Be extremely cautious when determining the eligibility of your company to be eligible for PF enrollment. The application for EPFO coverage must be submitted by the 30th day of achieving the threshold of twenty.

The application to register for PF is completed online and requires the digital signature of one director or the authorized signatory of the business. We help you determine the scope and validity of the PF and then submit your applications for registration on your behalf to the PF Office, draft all documents and follow up with the department to issue the number of registration for the company.

Very Important Point of Provident Fund (PF) Registration

Step 1. Mandatory Registration

An establishment with at least 20 employees has to register at the PF department within 30 days of attaining the number. This number is independent of the salary of employees.

Step 2. Voluntary Registration

With the employee's consent, employers may also request optional insurance coverage for their establishment to ensure that its employees can enjoy the fund for provident benefits.

Step 3. Meaning of Employees for PF

Full-time employees Part-Time Employees Employers Working from home Contract Employees/ Full-time Consultants.

Step 4.ESIC and PF Registration

The PF registration is only required when the employee count is 20 or more, so ESIC Registration is mandatory to be completed when the employee's total is reached 10.
