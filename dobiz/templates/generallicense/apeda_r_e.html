APEDA Sign-up

The agriculture and Processed Food Products Export Development Authority (APEDA) is an agency of the Government of India constituted under the Agriculture and Processed Food Products Export Development Authority Act, 1985 to facilitate the development of exports of scheduled products through financial assistance, information, and statistics and formulating standards, as well as taking action to facilitate the development of scheduled goods. The items that are specified in the APEDA ACT are referred to as Schedule products.

All exporters scheduled to export their product(s) must sign up with APEDA. The registration application must be submitted within one month of the date of agreement to export unless Authority can extend the period for registration with a valid reason. This is a once-only registration valid and in force until it is terminated through the Authority.

The goal of APEDA

APEDA aims to boost the export and development of scheduled products. To attain this goal, various tasks are delegated by central authorities to the agency. The central government controls the Authority of the body. The Authority is required to comply with the directives issued and follow the rules laid out by central authorities to assist in the purpose of ensuring the efficient administration of the Act. The Act constitutes an added law and does not constitute an amendment to the enforceable provisions.

The benefits of APEDA Registration

  • It's a requirement for exporters of scheduled goods.
  • It's a single-time registration.
  • It allows registered exporters to apply for financial aid under the schemes of APEDA.

Members who are registered can be a part of training courses that are organized across a variety of Industries that deal with scheduled items.
