Trade License

A trade license is a method to oversee a specific business in a specific location. It is controlled to ensure that the public isn't impacted negatively through Health Hazards & Nuisance by the unintentional carrying out of a business. Trade licenses are an instrument for ensuring that the method and location where the business operates are by applicable regulations and safety guidelines and standards. The state government sets the license for trade to oversee and regulate trade within cities. This license comes from the local municipal authority where the business is situated.

A trade license is authorized to conduct e-commerce or other business in the location for which it was granted. Unauthorized trading is a crime that could cause a severe penalty and subsequent prosecutorial action. The owners of the business must apply for a license to trade to be able to operate in the specific area without delay. A request must be submitted before the start of the business. Some state governments provide 15 to 3 months for the trade license application. Licenses issued after expiry require periodic renewal on an annual basis. Renewal applications should be made within 30 days of the license expiration. The procedure of Trade license registration is usually expensive and time-consuming.

Key Point of Trade License

Step. 1. Why do you need a Trade License?

To protect the general populace from health hazards and nuisances that the business or trader could create. Trade licenses are used to regulate trade and make sure that the rules are followed, and the government issues safety regulations.

Step 2. Licensing Authority for Trade License

Trading License(s) is issued by an authorized department in a city corporation in collaboration with other departments, such as the fire brigade, engineering, and health department that control the company.

Step 3. Commercial Premises

Trade Licenses are available only if the company is operated within the permitted zone. I.e., Commercial, Local commercial, Mixed Land Use, or Residential area with small shops of up to 20 sq.m. On the ground floor.

Step 4. Who requires a Trade License?

Any trader engaged in general trading activities within the territorial borders of any municipal corporation must be granted a trade license. The trade license type is different from state to state and is determined by the company's nature.

Step 5. The goal of a Trade License

A Trade License aims to control business places by restricting people from conducting certain kinds of businesses at home, thereby preserving the distinct business environment and protecting the local and the surrounding environment.

Step 6. State-specific application of the TL

Certain states have mandated an official trade license. In other states, it's an option. The type of license required and the cost of the license also vary between states. Some states have the fee monthly, while others require a revenue percentage.
