Overview FSSAI Central License

FSSAI Central License is a category of the highest license granted under the Food Safety and Standards Act 2006. The FBO, which operates a food business on a large scale, or generates more than 20,000,000 dollars annually, requires this license. Central Licensing authorities grant the Central Licensing license. FSSAI Central license contains a 14-digit code that begins at 100XXXXXXXXXXXXXXX.

FSSAI Central license is required by large FBOs or those specifically included, regardless of turnover. Here are the top-rated businesses that require FSSAI Central License.

FBO with the most common requirements for a Central License

The FBO with a more significant annual turnover than 20,000,000 annually must be exempted from consideration for the transporters. A transporter case has a limit of 30 crores in annual turnover to be eligible for a central license.

E-Commerce Operator: All E-Commerce companies, like Flipkart, Amazon and others, need a Central License. The central license does not require that sellers selling on such platforms have one.

The importer or exporter & units: FBOs operate through 100 percent Export Oriented Unites and those involved in food imports and exports, as well as food additives and ingredients for commercial purposes.

Multistate Operators: Food Business operators operating in more than one state need to obtain the required FSSAI Registration, License or License in each state and a Central License at Head Office.

Food Central licenses granted under these Regulations are valid for one to five years from the issue date unless canceled or suspended. The license issue date is considered the License's expiry date. It can be renewed up to five more years after the expiration date, subject to payment of the applicable fee.
The FBO is responsible for deciding the tenure they require for the central License. For each year, the FSSAI Central License Fee shall be Rs 7500.

FBO shall apply for a license renewal issued under these Regulations at least 30 days before the expiry date specified in the License. An applicant may file a renewal request 120 days in advance. The License will be valid when the renewal request is approved. Further, licensing authorities must decide on renewal within 30 calendar days of the expiry.
If required for reasons that need to be recorded in writing, designated officers may request an inspection before renewal. The Food Business Operator, who has a valid certificate from a Food Authority or accredited food safety auditor and any other organization notified by FSSAI, is exempted from inspection.
If the renewal request is not received within the validity period, then the License will expire, and the Food Business Operator must cease all business activity on the premises. If the Food Business Operator wants to restart it, they need to apply to renew it.
