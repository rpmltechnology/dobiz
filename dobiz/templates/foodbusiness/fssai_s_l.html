Validity and validity of FSSAI State License

A Food State license granted under these Regulations shall remain valid for 1-5 years as chosen by the Food Business Operator upon application unless canceled or temporarily suspended. The expiry date of the License is the date of issue. However, the License can be renewed for an additional five years, subject to payment for the applicable fee.

FBO must submit a request to renew a License granted under these Regulations at least 30 days before the License expires. An applicant can submit a renewal application up to 120 days prior. The License shall be valid until the disposal or renewal of the application. Further, the licensing authorities must determine the renewal within 30 calendar days of expiry. If they consider it necessary for reasons to be recorded in written form, the designated officers may order an inspector to inspect the License before renewal. The Food Business Operator with a valid certificate from a Food Authority accredited agency, a food safety auditor accredited, or any other FSSAI-notified organization is exempted from inspection.

Each day of delay in filing a renewal request shall result in a late fee equal to Rs 100 for every 30 days before the license expiry date. If the renewal request is not received within the validity period, the License shall be canceled, and all business activity at the premises will be stopped. If the Food Business Operator wants to restart it, it must apply to renew it.
