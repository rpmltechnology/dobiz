What is the definition of a Producer Company?

A producer company combines a cooperative organization and a Private Limited Company. It offers collective benefits to its members and operates under a strict and consistent legal framework. India permits only Private Limited Companies to register as Producer Companies. The names of these companies must be incorporated with the phrase "Producer Company Limited." An Indian Producer Company is a group of individuals or institutions involved in primary production activities.

Step-by-step procedure to register a private limited business

Producer Companies can register as Private Limited companies. Thus, registering Producer Companies is identical to that of Private Limited companies. Any Director of the Company can fill out the application. It is then submitted to the Registrar of Companies. This verifies the information in the application as well as the attached documents. After the verification, the ROC issues the Certificate of Incorporation in the company's name.

STEP 2 - Name Selection

A Producer Limited Company must have a unique name that conveys its brand and business activities. There are many requirements to create a Producer Limited Company. They include the Companies Act and Company (Incorporation) Rules. A company's company name cannot be identical to, or similar to, the name of an existing company, LLP, applied or registered trademark, or company (Incorporation) Rules, the Companies Act, the Trademark Act, and the Names and Emblems Act.

STEP 3- Name Approval, Reservation

It would help if you first chose a legally valid business name. Then, you must file it with the Registrar of Companies. This ensures that no other companies can use or steal it without your consent. Our name approval service will allow you to check the validity and apply for a reservation in the SPICe+ RUN (Reserve Uniform Name) or Part A.

STEP 4 - Filing of SPICe+ applications

After approval of its name, you may continue with the incorporation process for a Producer Limited Company. To apply for incorporation of the company, you will need to complete the SPICe+ application online. Part of SPICe+ is for reserving a name. Part B is for the Producer Company Register. Any producer company director can complete and sign this application before it is submitted to the ROC.

STEP 5- Issue of Certificates of Incorporation

After receiving the Producer Company application, the ROC verifies all documents and details. After confirming that the information and documents are authentic. The ROC will issue a Certificate to Incorporate into the company. The ROC also issues a Corporate Identification number as the company's unique identity.

Frequently Asked Questions

Producer Companies are companies involved directly or in part in Primary Production. These companies are generally classified as follows:
  • Production, procurement, or manufacture of primary produce by businesses
  • Companies involved in promotion/marketing primary produce
  • Companies that provide technical help to producers include those who train and educate members and research and development activities.
  • Businesses that only finance primary production activities
  • Businesses that provide a support infrastructure for primary business activities

All Members will receive the same value as the Producer Company in their initial payment. The Producer Company may also determine the value of any products or products that are pooled or supplied. Any withheld price can be disbursed later in cash, in person or equity shares. This is proportional to the Producer Company's production during the fiscal year.
Every Member receives a restricted return for the share capital contributed. If the surplus is not paid, it may be given as a patronage bonus to members in proportion to their participation in the Producer Company's business. The Members may decide to pay cash, allot equity shares, or both.

Producer companies must have their accounts books audited annually by a Chartered Accountant. These audits must be done at specified times as set out in the articles of incorporation. At the annual general meeting of shareholders, the Producer Company's auditor must be elected every year.

India's Producer Company registration process usually takes 20-25 business days.
