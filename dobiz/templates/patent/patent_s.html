Patentability Search

Patents confer an exclusive right to the patent for twenty years. The patent is granted once three essential requirements are met: novelty, utility and non-obviousness. Of these three important criteria used, novelty is the most significant one. The invention has to be unique to be eligible for the approval of a patent.

Prior research is the process of finding similar knowledge or writing by the invention. Because prior knowledge impedes Patentability issues for inventions, prior art search is the essential step before the inventor writes his specification or file for the patent. The earlier it's completed the better.

Prior art searches can uncover products that are concepts, inventions, or ideas similar to the idea or concept you'd like to patent. The most notable advantages of prior art search are under

  • Check it is Original and completely new.
  • It helps you understand what other people have filed in this field.
  • You have the opportunity to assess your invention against the similar prior art.
  • It lets you know the strength of your invention.
  • It protects you from violating the rights of someone else's invention.

Invalidity in Search

Invalidity Search is used to locate evidence that invalidates the patent filed by the opposing party. Also, the invalidity search is an approach to determine if the invention is legally binding or not. There are cases of positive use, for instance, if you're purchasing a patent or defending your invention in court, and the invalidity search plays a vital role in proving your argument. However, the negative use scenario is when you plan to invalidate someone else's patent. Invalidity searches are called validity searches and are utilized interchangeably across different jurisdictions. They are commonly used to evaluate the strength invalidity of the patent.

Freedom to Operate Search

Freedom to operate can also be called Clearance Search, Right to utilize search. Freedom to Practice Search identifies whether the invention in question may infringe on an earlier patent. The claims of an earlier similar patent are compared in conjunction with the description of the current patent to determine whether there's a potential violation. The freedom to operate search aims to minimize legal issues following the product's launch. Freedom to Operate searches is generally carried out in all countries before release.
