International Copyright Registration in India

Copyright rights of citizens of nations that have joined the Bern Convention for the Protection of Literary and Artistic Works, the Universal Copyright Convention and the TRIPS Convention are protected in India by the International Copyright Order.

Copyright rights, as defined in The Indian Copyright Act, are valid only within the boundaries of India. To protect Indian works that are used in other nations, India has become a part of the following international agreements on copyright as well as neighboring (related) rights

Berne Convention to protect Works of Art and Literature.

Universal Copyright Convention.

Artistic Works

Convention for the Protection of Producers of Phonograms against duplicates that are not authorized for the Phonograms.

Multilateral Convention for the avoidance of double taxation for Copyright royalties.

Trade-related aspects of the Intellectual Property Rights (TRIPS) Agreement
