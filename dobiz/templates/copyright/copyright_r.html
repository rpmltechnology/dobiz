Different types of Copyright

Literal Work

Literary creation was the first that could be written in any form, such as fiction or biography, papers or technical books, drama, script thesis, research tables, compilations, and computer programs, including computer databases. It is possible to claim it regardless of its style, quality or merit as a literary work.

Arts and Crafts Work

By article 2 in the Copyright Act, the Copyright in the artwork is granted to the original work of art, consisting of sculptures, paintings, graphic designs, cartoons, etchings, photographs, lithographs and diagrams, plans, maps and charts, building models and models of buildings. Molds and castings to make sculptures.

Dramatic Play

Dramatic work is an art form of literature that includes any work for reading, the arrangement for performing a play, choreographed or dumb show entertainment or a scenic arrangement, or an act in a set writing work. But it is not a cinematograph film.

Tone Recording

Songs that contain a singer's voice, whether without or with music or a recorded speech podcast, are examples of recording sound. This covers any task that records sound, regardless of the medium or storage. If the recording contains music, then permission from the music composer must be obtained.

Music Works

Music is considered a copyright-protected work by itself and doesn't contain any lyrics or audio. The sounds of the recording are dependent on music; however, to safeguard musical works, separate applications are required to be filed along alongside the music work. The creator of the sound recording must seek permission from the creator of the music work.

Cinematograph Film

Cinematography is the work of recording visuals and sound recording made possible through any method, digital or analog-like video films. It is a visual recording on any medium and using any means of storage. According to the definition, any recorded film containing moving images/visuals can be considered a cinematograph.

Application Filing

The application must be submitted along with 2 copies of the document and the prescribed government fee, ranging between 500 to Rs. 500 up to Rs. 5000 based on the work.

Copyright Examination

Once the application is completed, the copyright department will wait for 30 days and then issue an examination report that accepts the application with objections.
