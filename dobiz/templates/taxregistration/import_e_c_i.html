Introduction to Import Export Code

Import Export Cod (IEC) is a 10-digit code alphanumeric that is given to businesses to import or export products or services. Without the Import and Export Code, no business can import or export. The Regional Office of the Director General of Foreign Trade can issue the IEC. It's used to uniquely identify Indian import and export companies by tax authorities such as Customs and DGFT officers in India. The DGFT can also use the IEC code to regulate Indian businesses importing and exporting activities and to ensure the financial health and sustainability of Indian economies.

How to apply for Import Export Code

The registration process for IEC registration is simple and easy. It can also be done 100% online. Visit the official website at DGFT to register your business entity. After registering on the website successfully, you can complete the application for IEC Registration. You must attach all necessary documents, pay the prescribed application fees, and then submit the application form. A registration certificate will be sent along with an allotment IEC after your business has been registered for IEC. You can then use this certificate to conduct foreign trade activities.

STEP 1- Documentation

Before beginning the registration for an IEC code, it is essential to organize all necessary documents. Below is a list containing all the documents required to be submitted with your application. Your entire application might be rejected if you fail to provide any of these required documents or file false documents. You should arrange all documents in advance to avoid any confusion or delay in the application.

STEP 2 – Go to the DGFT website

To register for an IEC, visit the official website of the Director General for Foreign Trade (DGFT). To access the homepage, click on the "Services" tab. Next, select the "IEC profile management" option from the dropdown. After clicking on this option, you will be directed to registration.

STEP 3 – Register for the DGFT site

To register, go to the new page and click on the option "Apply for IEC." After that, click on the option "Register." The registration form will be open on the screen. You should finish up fundamental subtleties such as name, organization, contact numbers, messages, and precise location.

STEP 4 – Conduct OTP-based validation

After you have completed the registration form, you will be asked to generate an OTP to confirm your contact details. The OTP will come to your email ID or mobile number. Once they have been validated, you will receive a temporary password that you can use to log in and fill in the detailed application for online import/export registration.

STEP 5 Login and complete an application form

You will use your temporary password to log in to the DGFT site and complete the application. Attach or upload the required documents and pay the applicable application fees. Once your application has been completed, you can click "submit" and wait until your application is processed. You will need to fill out the ANF 2A application.

STEP 6 – Issuance of IEC Code and IEC Certificate of Registration

After the IEC registration application is processed successfully, the DGFT issues an Import Export Code to each applicant. The registration certificate serves as proof that the applicant has registered IEC. Only after receiving the IEC Code and the registration Certificate can the applicant import and export and conduct legal foreign trade.
