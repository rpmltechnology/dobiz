Professional Tax Registration

Professional Tax (PTA) is an income tax levied by certain states in India for those earning salaries or practicing professionals like accountants, Chartered Accountants, Company Secretaries, Doctors, Lawyers etc., and from companies, LLPs, Directors and Designated Partners.

Professional Tax RegistrationServices in India

Every professional such as the Directors of the Company, Designated Partners of the LLP or any other employer (which may be the Company as well as LLP) is obliged to register as stated above and ensure that professional tax deductions are included in the pay of employees.

The professional tax deducted from the employees should be paid to the appropriate office that the government of your state designates, and a return of the professional tax must be submitted indicating the tax payment for professional employees.

Every employer must deduct and pay taxes to employees on their behalf. For this reason, the employer must be able to get the registration document from the department of professional tax within 30 days of the date on which he is liable.

Application of Professional Tax Registration

Step 1.Firm / Company / LLP

The individuals like HUF or companies, Limited Liability Partnerships (LLP), Companies, Corporations and other corporate bodies, clubs, societies or associations are considered tax-paying individuals under the Professional Tax Act. Or every branch of these people is considered a distinct person for registration in The Professional Tax Department and Tax payment.

Step 2. Individual Professionals

Legal Practitioners, including Solicitors and Notaries; Medical Representatives, including medical Consultants and Dentists; Doctors and chartered accountants; Company Secretaries, Managers Consultants; Architectures, Engineers and Tax Consultants; Insurance Agents and Surveyors; all kinds of Contractors and others.

Step 3. Directors & Partners

The Directors of companies or the Partner of a company or designated Partner, as well as Partners of LLP, are also considered professionals. To meet the Professional Tax law requirements, the Directors and Partners must register under the PT Act within 30 days of the day they are appointed.

Step 4. As an Employer

The tax for professionals who are salaried employees in the state of their employment is collected by the employer following the proper deduction from the employees' wages and then deposited with the professional's tax department. The employer must apply for approval from the professional tax department 30 days from the date of application.
