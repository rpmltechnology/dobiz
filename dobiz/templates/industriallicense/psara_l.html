Who can apply to apply for Psara License online in India?

PSARA License is required for anyone who wants to start an enterprise in private security in India. Private Security Agency: Private Security Agency is an organization that offers Private Security Guards and other related services to the establishment as an alternative in the absence of the police. Private security agencies' operations are controlled by the Private Security Agencies Regulation Act 2005, which is enforced through the Department of Internal Security of the Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India.

Since the enactment of the PSARA (Act), it is now mandatory for a Security Company to obtain a License through the State Controlling Authority Concerned before launching the business. Additionally, in the event of non-compliance, the Law imposes severe sanctions. The application for a PSARA License to start a private security company is submitted to the appropriate authority of the state. This PSARA License is issued to be able to operate for one or more districts in one particular state or the whole state.

  • Legal Regulations
  • Legal: The Private Security Agencies (Regulation) Act, 2005
  • Rules Privacy Security Agencies Central Model Rules, 2006
  • Effective Date: 14th March 2006.

State Govt Role The State Government Role is part of the central Law; however, under section 3 in PSARA, each state is required by Law to name an officer who is not below an officer of rank Joint Secretary as the authority in charge via a notification.

Liability of License It is necessary to note that the PSARA License remains valid for five years from the date of issue or renewal. The renewal application for the Psara license must be filed at least 45 days before the license expiry date. If the License expires, a renewal application would be required.

FDI in PSARA

Section 6(2) of the Private Security Agencies (Regulation) Act in 2005 spells out the conditions for the PSARA License not issued if the applicant isn't legally registered in India or has an owner or a majority shareholder director, partner, or shareholder that is not a citizen of India. Foreign direct investment into the Private Security Agency business is permitted at a maximum of 49% in the form of a Company or LLP through the approval route. The application to approve FDI is managed through the Ministry of Home Affairs of the federal government. Therefore, before the application to grant a PSARA License is made by a business with foreign investments, it must have prior approval of FDI. The relevant reports, such as FC-GPR, must be submitted and approved.

Step-by-Step Method of Psara

Documentation

The proper documentation is required to make the process smooth, and the list of documents needed is based on the kind of applicant.

MOU with Training Institute

The applicant must sign the Memorandum of Understanding with a recognized training institution to instruct the guards and supervisors. Every state has recognized various security training institutions to train security guards and supervisors.

Application Filing

The application for granting a PSARA License is filed before the state's authority in charge. A separate application has to be submitted for each state, as there is no requirement for a single central license that covers all states.

Verification

Following the submission of the form, the background of the business as well as its principal officers or directors is verified through authorities at the Local Police station having jurisdiction at an address that is provided by the person who filed the application or director or principal officer(s) depending on what the situation is.

PSARA License Issue PSARA License

After a thorough examination of the documents as well as the applicant's previous antecedent after which, the Controlling Authority issues the PSARA License. In certain instances, the Controlling Authority may require a personal interview with the applicant.

State Level Variation

The process of granting a PSARA License is more or more or less the same across all states in India. However, there are minor differences in how documentation is prepared and presented because officers are state-owned subjects; we found that the process for antecedent confirmation of the principal, director or applicant officer differs from state to state.
