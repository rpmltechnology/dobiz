DoT OSP License Application online in India

Speck stands for Branch of Media transmission, and OSP refers to Other Specialist organizations. A new telecom policy (NTP) in 1999 made the requirement for specific types of businesses, also referred to under the term Other Service Providers (OSP), to get a License before launching a company, and a business can't operate without an approved license. Service providers who use telecom services are classified as OSP. This includes BPO and KPO Services, Voice-based or non-voice-based Call Centers, E-Commerce Companies, Tele-banking and Telemedicine, Tele-education, and other IT-supported services.

Only companies registered under the Companies Act 2013 can apply to grant a DOT OSP License and access additional telecom services and infrastructure designed for communications purposes from licensed telecom providers that the DOT has authorized to be able to do this.

Businesses that require OSP License from DOT

Step 1: Phonebanking

Banks offer this type of service to their customers. Customers can conduct transactions without visiting the bank branch and in a paper-free environment.

Step 2: Telemedicine

It is a new method for delivering medical care to patients. Emergency health services and guidelines are given to the patient via telephone or online.

Step 3: Tele Education

This is a method to provide knowledge in remote areas using the help of telecommunication equipment to people living in remote areas.

Step 4: TeleCommerce

The vendor advertises the items or services to customers through a communications medium. This includes sales via television shows.

Step 5: Call Center

All call centers refer to businesses that handle many calls for commercial purposes. This also covers all types of BPO and KPO Services.

Step 6: Network operation center

They are typically established through Internet Service Providers to supervise and manage the network traffic within a particular region.

Frequently Asked Questions

The business that has been awarded a DOT OSP License must submit an annual status report with a list of activities performed in the preceding year and the present state. This is essential to keep this OSP License valid.

It is important to note that the DOT OSP License is valid for the period it was issued. The duration of validity is listed on the License. In general, the DOT OSP License is issued for 20 years. However, for it to remain valid, the business must file an annual status report.

As per the telecom policy according to telecom policy, the DOT OSP License issued to one particular company cannot be transferred to any other business.

The company's annual return that was granted the License must be filed within 6 months of the date of the financial year.

If the annual report is not submitted at the time of an OSP, then the business is removed from the dormant lists of the OSP, which means that the License will be removed after two years.
