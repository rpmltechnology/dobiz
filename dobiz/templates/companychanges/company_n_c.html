Preliminary note on the name change of the company

The company's name represents its unique identity as a private limited corporation. This first clause of MOA (also known under the Name Clause) is about the company's Name. If the management wants to change the company's Name, they must get the consent of their shareholders and get approval from the ROC. A change in the company's Name requires approval at four stages. Here are the requirements for prior approval to change the company name.

  • Approval of the Board of Directors
  • Approval by more than 75% of shareholders
  • Approval for the ROC, MGT-14
  • Approval for the Central Government

Legal Provision. All companies, no matter how old or new, are allowed to file a request for a name change. Section 13 (2), Companies Act (Incorporation) Rules, 2014, does not allow for name changes if a company has not filed ROC Annual Report, Financial Statement, or any other report/documents with ROC on the due date.

Common Reasons to Change Company Name

Voluntary Name Change

The company can, at its discretion, change the Name of the company. But, it must meet all other conditions.

Change in Business Activity

The company could decide to change the Name of its business to reflect these new or more business objects. The company might also alter its MOA to change the main object in this case.

Rebranding/Marketing

Sometimes, companies may change their name to market the company or position it better.

Change of Ownership

As a result, it is common for the company name to change after a change of Ownership or takeover. This happens to reflect the authority and branding of the new management.

To Avoid IPR Issues

Some companies change their names to strengthen their trademarks. A company name may be changed as well to avoid any IPR Conflict.

Respecting the ROC Instruction

The ROC has the power to make an order directing a company's change in Name following a complaint from any company claiming priority for the use of a Name or Trademark.

Change of company name in a step-wise process

Held Board meeting

The board approves the proposed Name of the directors.

Fill out a RUN Application

After the board of directors has determined its new Name and approved it, a request to reserve a new name is made under the RUN (Reserve Unique Names) facility at the ROC. Also included in the RUN Application is a copy of the Board Resolution and a NOC from the Trademark Owner (if applicable). The government fee for filing a RUN application is Rs. 1000/-. The ROC approves a name if it is distinctive and not identical to an existing company, LLP domain, or trademark.

EGM

Once approval has been received from the office registrar companies for the Name, the next step is to ask the shareholders for their approval. A notice must be sent to each shareholder by the directors at least 21 days before the EGM. The notice must contain the draft of any proposed resolution and an explanatory statement.

Filing MGT-14 - Registration of Special Resolution

The particular resolution refers to a decision by more than 75% of voting rights. The particular resolution is adopted in the EGM and filed with the required fee before the ROC.

Filing INC-24 (Seeking Central Government Approval)

With an amended copy of MOA & AOA, a Copy of Notice EGM, and an application to change the company's Name, Form INC-24 is completed. The company's new Name is added to the Certificate of Incorporation.

Issue of a new Certificate of Incorporation issued by ROC

MGT-14 and INC-24 approval, the ROC issues a brand new Certificate of Incorporation. The company is required to update the existing Name everywhere instead of the old Name Everywhere.

Documents Required to Change Company Name

You will need two sets of documents to file a request for the name change of an existing company. The company will have the first set of papers, while the second set includes the drafts necessary for the name changes. Once the documents have been signed, they are annexed to the relevant ROC Form, which can include MGT-14, RUN Form and INC-24.
