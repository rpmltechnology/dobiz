For-Profit Business Types

Proprietorship

This is for small-scale businesses with a limited budget, such as small stores. Could open their shop as a sole proprietorship company. It's a single-person operation with all the profits and losses going to the sole proprietor. Proprietorship companies are not registered businesses. But, other registrations could be applicable.

Partnership

A traditional form of business is not widely used and isn't relevant. You can establish an association company by signing the partnership deed. Its registration is not required, but it is required since an unregistered business cannot recuperate more than 100 rupees. 100 from its creditors.

Limited Liability Partnership

An LLP is a step up from the traditional partnership companies and has some distinctive characteristics of a business, such as the limit on liability. The procedure of incorporating the LLP is a bit complicated, and at the same time, it's not equipped to expand and isn't recommended for financing.

One Person Company

Now, you can create an organization with only one director and shareholder. It is necessary to have another person be your nominee. But the OPC must be converted to an open or public company when its capital exceeds 50 Lacs and its turnover exceeds 2 Crores.
