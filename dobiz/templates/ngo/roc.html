NGO Registration by CSR-1

India-based companies must adhere to corporate social responsibility. CSR can be done by such companies themselves or through NGOs. CSR grants are available to NGOs from corporate bodies to carry out CSR activities. CSR grants can be received from body corporate to enable them to carry out CSR activities on their behalf.

India CSR Registration Online

Companies are required to contribute voluntarily to the community's welfare. Companies can secure this contribution in their CSR funds. These funds may be used by the body corporate that keeps them or outsourced to non-profit organizations which will perform CSR activities on behalf of the body corp.

For this purpose, grants may be granted to NGOs from the CSR fund of body corporations if they are "Bodies undertaking CSR actions" registered with the ROC. The regulations regarding the registration of NGOs to obtain CSR funding and engage in CSR activities were introduced on April 1, 2021.

Eligible NGOs for CSR registration

Non-profit, non-governmental organizations usually engage in activities for the advancement and welfare of society. Therefore they are well-suited to take on CSR activities that are outsourced to them by corporate bodies subject to corporate social accountability. Corporations shall provide CSR funds to these NGOs to outsource CSR. This will require NGOs to register with them before they can grant CSR funds. Here are the lists of NGOs that are eligible for CSR activities.

List of NGO eligible for CSR Register

  • Section 8 companies may be registered under Section 12 A and Section 80 of Income Tax Act
  • Registered Society also registered in Section 12A and Section 80 of the Income Tax Act
  • Registered public trusts may also be registered under Section 12 A of the Income Tax Act and Section 80.
  • A registered society or registered trust is a legal entity that has been established by the Central or State governments
  • You should note that all of the entities above must have a track-record of undertaking at minimum 3 years of allowed social welfare activities.

CSR Registration is a step-by-step process for NGOs

CSR registration requires that NGOs file online Form CSR 1 and submit it along with the required documents to the Registrar of Companies. Below, you will find a complete explanation of CSR registration.

Procedure for obtaining CSR registration

STEP 1 - Documentation

Documentation is what will determine whether or not the CSR application is approved. You should organize all necessary documents and submit them with your application to avoid confusion or CSR registration delays.

STEP 2, Filing Form CSR1

On the MCA's website, you can submit your application for CSR registration CSR 1. The online application can be completed with all information requested. The Form is available online and can be signed by

STEP3-Certification of Form CSR1

Once completed, the Form must first be certified by an independently practicing CA, CS, and CMA. This ensures that the information and documents included in the application are 100% authentic.

STEP4: Submission of CSR1

Once the CSR 1 Form has been completed by an authorized professional and is certified, it can be uploaded online to the MCA portal. Ensure the documents you upload with your CSR application are self-attested by the applicant.

STEP 5. - Registration CSR

After the submission of Form CSR1, the RoC thoroughly reviews the application. The RoC issues a unique CSR registration ID to the NGO. It then becomes eligible to apply for CSR grants in India and engage in CSR activities.
