What does Trusts mean?

Indian Trusts Act 1882 defines a trust as an obligation that is annexed through the ownership or use of the property. Stepwise documents required for trust registration in India

The Registrar of Trusts India handles the online registration for NGO Trusts. While private trusts can be registered according to the Indian Trusts Act (1882), public trusts must be registered according to the Trust Act of the state where they are being established. The entire Trust Registration process revolves around the signing and signing of a Trust Deed. This is done in the presence of a registrar and two witnesses. Below is a step-by-step guide to the registration of Trusts India.

STEP 1 - Documentation

The proper submission of all documents required by law is a prerequisite for Trust Registration in India. Inadequate or incorrect documentation may lead to the rejection of the entire Trust Registration application. Therefore, arranging all documents ahead of time is essential to avoid any hassles or hindrances during NGO Trust registration online.

STEP 2 - Write your Trust Deed

Trust deeds are essential documents for Trust registration. It is what lays the foundation for Trust formation in India. A trust document must be prepared with care, following the legal requirements. Once the Trust Deed has been completed, it can be submitted to the Office of the Registrar of Trusts along with any required documents. This will begin the process for Trust Registration.

STEP 3. - Name Selection

The name of the Trust must be unique and should communicate the purpose for which it was created. It should not be the name of any Trust or NGO, an application trademark or a registered trademark. It should not be identical to an already established Trust's name. However, a name identical or similar to a registered trademark or trademark will be considered invalid to name your Trust.

STEP4: Application for Trust Registry

The Trust Deed registration is the first step towards Trust Registration. After the deed is drafted, it is sent to the Office of the Registrar of Trust. The documents should include photographs of the settler and their proofs of identity. After inspecting the deed and confirming its authenticity, the registrar asks the settler to appear in his presence with two witnesses. After the deed is signed, the registry will begin registering NGO Trusts online.

STEP5: Issuance of Trust Registration Certificate

The Certificate of Trust registration is issued to register Trust. The Certificate of Registration proves that the Trust has been correctly registered. The Trust Registration process in India takes approximately a week.
